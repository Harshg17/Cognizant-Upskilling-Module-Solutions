Community Bulletin

Summer Festival Preparations Underway

The annual Summer Festival committee has begun preparations for the biggest event of the year. Volunteers are needed across multiple departments including logistics, food coordination, and stage management. This year's festival will feature over 30 local artists and craftspeople, with dedicated zones for children's activities and senior citizens. Early bird registrations are now open with a 20% discount.

The event will span three days across the main city park, with dedicated zones for children's activities, food stalls, live performances, and an artisan marketplace. Community feedback from last year has been incorporated into the new layout design. Parking arrangements have been expanded with a new shuttle service from downtown.

New Workshop Series Launched

The city council is proud to announce a new monthly workshop series focused on digital skills for senior residents. Topics will include smartphone basics, online banking safety, video calling with family, and social media literacy. The workshops are completely free and will be held every second Saturday at the Community Center.

Instructors are drawn from local tech companies who have volunteered their time to support the initiative. Registration is required as spots are limited to 20 participants per session to ensure personalized attention. Refreshments will be provided. Contact the council office to reserve your spot or register online through this portal.
